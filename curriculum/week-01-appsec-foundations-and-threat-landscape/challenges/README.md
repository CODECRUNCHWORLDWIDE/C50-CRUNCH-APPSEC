# Week 1 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems. Unlike the exercises, these have **no single right answer graded by row count** — the skill is judgment and clear writing. Do them after the three exercises.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Write a rules-of-engagement document](challenge-01-write-a-rules-of-engagement.md)** — the formal document that turns "I have a lab" into "I have authorization, scope, and legality, in writing." *(~45 min.)*
2. **[Challenge 2 — Attacker/defender story](challenge-02-attacker-defender-story.md)** — take one real vulnerability class and tell its story twice: once from the attacker's side, once from the defender's. *(~45 min.)*

## How these are judged

There's no answer key. Each challenge tells you what a *strong* submission looks like. You're being graded on:

- **Precision** — does your document say exactly what's authorized, by whom, on what, and for how long — no vague hand-waving?
- **The attacker/defender habit** — can you hold both sides of a vulnerability in your head at once, the core skill Lecture 1 introduced?
- **Honesty about limits** — do you flag what you're *not* sure of, rather than papering over gaps with confident-sounding prose?

Keep your work in `challenge-01.md` and `challenge-02.md`. The reasoning is the point — a document with no reasoning behind it, even a technically correct one, is an incomplete answer.
