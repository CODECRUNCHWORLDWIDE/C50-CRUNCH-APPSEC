# Week 11 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems. Unlike the exercises, these have **no answer key with a fixed finding count** — the skill is judgment and thoroughness, not recall. Do them after the three exercises.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Full review of a lab app](challenge-01-full-review-of-a-lab-app.md)** — review *all* of `crunch-invoices`, main branch included, with the complete four-step method and no hand-holding. *(~90 min.)*
2. **[Challenge 2 — Review vs. scanner bake-off](challenge-02-review-versus-scanner.md)** — run Week 8's scanner against the same app and compare its output to your manual findings, honestly. *(~90 min.)*

## How these are judged

There's no row-count answer key. Instead, each challenge tells you what a *thorough* submission looks like. You're being graded on:

- **Completeness of the inventory** — did you actually enumerate every route and every sink, or did you stop once you'd found "enough"?
- **Explicit "no finding" statements** — a real review states, for code you checked and found clean, that you checked it and why it's clean — not just silence.
- **Honesty in the comparison** (Challenge 2) — did you report where the scanner beat you, not just where you beat it?

Keep your work in `challenge-01.md` / `challenge-02.md` plus the databases and diffs each one asks for. The reasoning is the point.
